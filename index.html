Biography

A Rutherford County record with deep local roots

Representative Tim Rudd was raised in Murfreesboro and Smyrna, graduated from Smyrna High School, and attended Middle Tennessee State University. Before serving in the Tennessee House, he built a career as a real estate agent, direct mail consultant, and small business owner, giving him a practical understanding of budgets, regulation, property, and the pressures facing working families.

Rudd first became involved in public life after a local annexation dispute threatened a neighbor's property. That experience shaped the approach he has carried into office: defend property rights, keep government accountable, and make sure ordinary citizens are not pushed aside by bureaucracy.

His service is also personal. After his father passed away in 1984, Rudd helped support his mother. Years later, he served as her caregiver as she battled dementia until her passing at age 91. That experience continues to inform his work on senior issues, caregiver concerns, and family-centered public policy.

Rudd's public service reflects the place that shaped him: steady, neighborly, practical, and proud of Rutherford County's growth without losing sight of its hometown character.

Priorities

Focused on the issues that matter at home

Representative Rudd's work in Nashville is shaped by the conversations he has with families, business owners, seniors, teachers, and taxpayers across District 34.

Election integrity

As chair of the Elections and Campaign Finance Subcommittee, Rudd has focused on secure elections, transparent rules, and public confidence in the process.

Property rights

From his first involvement in local government, Rudd has treated private property as a core constitutional issue and a practical concern for homeowners and small businesses.

Small business

With decades in the private sector, Rudd understands that Tennessee grows stronger when entrepreneurs can work, hire, invest, and build without unnecessary red tape.

Senior issues

Rudd's own experience as a caregiver gives him a direct understanding of the pressure families face when caring for aging parents and loved ones with dementia.

Local government

Local decisions should be open, accountable, and fair to taxpayers. Rudd has consistently pushed for government that answers to citizens, not insiders.

Public safety

Safe neighborhoods are the foundation of a strong community. Rudd supports law enforcement, first responders, and policies that hold offenders accountable.

Constituent Service

Helping District 34 navigate state government

Representative Rudd's office is available to help constituents with state agency questions, legislative concerns, state services, and issues affecting Rutherford County families.

Constituent service is part of the job. Whether the question comes from a longtime Smyrna family, a Murfreesboro small business owner, a senior caregiver, or a new neighbor settling into District 34, the goal is the same: listen, respond, and help where state government is involved.
